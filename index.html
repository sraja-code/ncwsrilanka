About the NCW

Who We Are

The National Commission on Women is Sri Lanka's newest independent commission — a landmark institution dedicated to protecting and promoting the rights of all women in the country.

Our Mandate

Established to Protect and Promote Women's Rights

The NCW is established under the Women Empowerment Act No. 37 of 2024 — a significant milestone in the legislative advancement of women's rights in Sri Lanka. The seven Commissioners were appointed by H.E. the President Anura Kumara Dissanayake in September 2025, based on the recommendations of the Constitutional Council and the Women Parliamentarians' Caucus.

As an independent entity, the Commission has oversight powers over the Ministry of Women and Child Affairs and the implementation of the Sri Lanka National Policy on Gender Equality and Women's Empowerment, approved by Cabinet and launched in March 2023.

The Women Empowerment Act sets out a foundational commitment that "every woman shall be entitled to women's rights and no woman shall be denied of such rights" (Section 3). The NCW is mandated to give full effect to this commitment.

Objectives of the Women Empowerment Act

  • Introduce mechanisms to secure women's rights subject to the Constitution
  • Give effect to Sri Lanka's obligations under CEDAW and applicable laws
  • Formulate a national policy for the advancement and empowerment of women
  • Protect women from all forms of discrimination based on gender and sexual orientation
  • Prevent discrimination, marginalisation, sexual harassment, and violence against women
  • Make recommendations for law reforms to prevent discrimination and ensure empowerment
  • Promote women's participation in justice mechanisms and equal representation in decision-making
  • Ensure the economic empowerment of women, with special regard to women with disabilities
  • Conduct public education programmes on all aspects of women's empowerment
  • Promote revision of laws ensuring equal salaries for work of equal value

Powers of the Commission (Section 6)

  • Inquire into and investigate infringement or imminent infringement of women's rights
  • Intervene in court proceedings relating to women's rights with the permission of the Court
  • Conduct public inquiries and institute actions in any competent Court on its own
  • Refer women subject to discrimination for mediation, on its own motion or on complaint
  • Request information and reports from any person or institution
  • Liaise and interact with state institutions to foster common policies and promote co-operation in handling complaints
  • Monitor and evaluate adherence by private persons and non-state institutions to women's rights standards
  • Make recommendations to Government on aligning domestic law with international norms
  • Assess the impact of legislation, policies, and practices on women's rights
  • Formulate a National Policy and framework on the advancement and empowerment of women
  • Award compensation to aggrieved persons for expenses reasonably incurred in making a complaint
  • Open regional and provincial offices of the Commission across Sri Lanka
  • Conduct awareness programmes and undertake research on women's rights
  • Receive grants, gifts, and donations from local and foreign sources

Inquiry, Investigation and Enforcement (Part II)

  • Supreme Court references: The Commission receives references from the Supreme Court on matters relating to women's rights arising under Article 126 of the Constitution, for inquiry and report (Section 17)
  • Supreme Court determinations: The Commission may refer questions on the scope of women's rights to the Supreme Court for determination during any inquiry (Section 17(3))
  • Own-motion investigations: The Commission may investigate any infringement of women's rights on its own initiative, without waiting for a complaint. Such investigations must be concluded within one month (Section 18)
  • Complaints: Any aggrieved person, or someone acting on her behalf, may submit a complaint. The NCW must inform the complainant of action taken within 14 days of its decision (Section 19)
  • Mediation: Disputes may be referred to a trained panel of mediators. Mediation must be concluded within 60 days (Section 20)
  • Appeals: Any person who does not accept the Commission's decision may appeal to the Court of Appeal within one month (Section 26)
  • Non-compliance: Any person who fails to comply with the Commission's recommendations or decisions is guilty of contempt of the Commission. The Commission may transmit a Certificate of such determination to the Supreme Court (Section 30)

Legal Basis

The NCW is established under the Women Empowerment Act No. 37 of 2024, certified on 2 July 2024 and published in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.

Appointment of Commissioners

Seven Commissioners were appointed in September 2025 by H.E. the President — five on the recommendation of the Constitutional Council (following a public nomination and interview process) and two on the recommendation of the Women Parliamentarians' Caucus. At least five members, including the Chairperson, must be women. Commissioners serve four-year terms commencing September 2025.

Under Section 5(3), Commissioners must be persons of distinction in public life with proven knowledge and experience in law, legislation, trade unionism, management, administration, economic development, health, education, or women's empowerment.

The Ombuds

The Act provides for the appointment of an Ombuds, empowered to receive complaints connected with gender-based violence or infringement of women's rights, and to forward her recommendations to the Commission.

National Fund for Women

The Act establishes a National Fund for Women, administered by the Commission. The Fund receives parliamentary allocations and can accept donations and grants from local and foreign sources. It may be used to make ex-gratia payments to individuals in recognition of outstanding contributions to women's rights.

How to Submit a Complaint

The NCW is mandated to receive and investigate complaints regarding violations of women's rights. Any complaint may be made by an aggrieved person, or by any person acting on her behalf.

Key timelines under the Act:

  • 1 month to submit a complaint from the date of the infringement
  • 14 days for the NCW to inform you of the action taken on your complaint
  • 60 days for mediation to be concluded if referred to mediation
  • 1 month to appeal a decision of the Commission to the Court of Appeal

Please contact us by email or post to submit a complaint. The NCW also has the power to investigate matters on its own initiative.
