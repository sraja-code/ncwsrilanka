About the NCW

Who We Are

The National Commission on Women is Sri Lanka's newest independent commission — a landmark institution dedicated to protecting and promoting the rights of all women in the country.

Our Mandate

Established to Protect and Promote Women's Rights

The NCW is established under the Women Empowerment Act No. 37 of 2024, a significant milestone in the legislative advancement of women's rights protection in Sri Lanka. The seven Commissioners were appointed by H.E. the President Anura Kumara Dissanayake in September 2025, based on the recommendations of the Constitutional Council and the Women Parliamentarians' Caucus.

As an independent commission, the NCW has been mandated with wide powers to protect and promote women's rights — including the power to inquire into and investigate alleged violations, resolve grievances through mediation, and formulate a National Policy on the advancement and empowerment of women.

We acknowledge, with deep appreciation, that the Women Empowerment Act of 2024 and the NCW are the culmination of decades of struggle and perseverance by women's rights collectives and organisations islandwide.

Our Key Functions

  • Inquire into and investigate alleged violations of women's rights
  • Resolve grievances through mediation between parties
  • Formulate a National Policy on the advancement and empowerment of women
  • Conduct awareness programmes on women's rights and the WE Act
  • Undertake research and disseminate knowledge on women's rights
  • Make recommendations to Government on aligning domestic law with international standards
  • Assess the impact of proposed laws and policies on women's rights
  • Issue public statements on critical issues affecting women
  • Liaise with state institutions, non-state actors, and civil society

Legal Basis

The NCW is established under the Women Empowerment Act No. 37 of 2024, certified on 2 July 2024 and published in the Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.

Appointment

Seven Commissioners were appointed in September 2025 by H.E. the President, on the recommendation of the Constitutional Council and the Women Parliamentarians' Caucus.

Current Status

The NCW is actively commencing its primary functions. Due to limited capacity, the inquiry and investigative functions are being progressively operationalised.

International Frameworks

International Instruments

UN Treaty · Ratified by Sri Lanka

CEDAW Convention

The Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women. Sri Lanka's ninth periodic report was reviewed by the UN CEDAW Committee in February 2025.

Beijing Platform for Action

The comprehensive international framework for women's rights and gender equality, adopted at the Fourth World Conference on Women in 1995. A key reference for NCW policy work.
